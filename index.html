ü§ù Restorative Practices & Ladder of Referral

School-wide Community Agreements

  • We treat everyone with dignity and respect
  • Honesty, integrity, and kindness
  • Be responsible to yourself, others, and the school community
  • Everyone has a voice and belongs here
  • Be proactive with your actions, attitudes, and beliefs
  • Talk it out and find win-win solutions

Ladder of Referral Process

  1. Try in-class strategies first
  2. Seek support from Grade Leader or Department Chair
  3. Refer to PPS Team: Dean Grant, Counselor King, Social Worker Heyward
  4. Escalate to Assistant Principal: George (Grades 5-8) / Petot (Grades 3K-4)
  5. Principal Charlemagne for unresolved issues

Important: No shouting or placing students in hallways. Use compassionate correction and follow the ladder before requesting classroom removal.

üì± Distraction-Free Learning Policy (Effective Sept 3, 2025)

New NYS Legislation & Chancellor's Regulation A-413

All public schools must now implement policies limiting student access to internet-enabled personal devices during instructional hours.

Collection & Storage Procedures

  • Students turn off ALL internet-enabled devices upon arrival
  • Devices placed in designated collection bins
  • Devices returned at dismissal (2:20 PM regular / 5:30 PM after-school)
  • Late arrivals/early dismissals follow check-in/check-out protocols
  • School not responsible for uncollected devices

Updated Progressive Consequences

  • 1st Offense: Device held until dismissal
  • 2nd Offense: Parent must retrieve device Wednesdays 2:00-4:00 PM only
  • 3rd+ Offense: Parent pickup Wednesdays 2:00-4:00 PM + meeting with leadership

Exception Requests

Contact Assistant Principal Petot at 718-922-3505. Reviewed within 5-7 school days with official documentation.

Device Access Times

Before School: 7:30-8:00 AM | After School: 2:20 PM / 5:30 PM after-school

üö® Safety & Emergency Procedures

Door Alarms & Building Security

  • All doors equipped with alarms - remain alarmed at all times
  • Staff use ONLY main entrance for entering/exiting
  • If door alarm sounds: Report immediately to main office (ext. 1000)
  • Emergency button in each classroom - notify office of emergency

Security Codes

  • Soft/Hard Lockdown: Keep doors locked, windows covered, account for all students
  • "Code Blue (room #)": AED-CPR emergency (for responders only)
  • "Lockdown lifted": Resume normal activities

Emergency Drill Procedures

  • Carry evacuation folder and attendance records
  • Students maintain absolute silence
  • Follow posted evacuation routes
  • Trained AED-CPR responders available in building

üìù Lesson Planning & Classroom Setup

Required Lesson Plan Elements

  • Learning Standard being addressed
  • Learning Targets & Success Criteria (posted on whiteboard)
  • Differentiation/grouping strategies
  • Materials and concept development
  • Assessment/evaluation/exit tickets
  • Higher order thinking questions

Classroom Display Requirements

  • Whiteboard: Daily schedule, school heading, learning targets, success criteria
  • Door: Teacher name and class sign (windows NOT covered except during drills)
  • Bulletin boards: Current student work, reflect standards, change every 5-6 weeks
  • Emergency procedures posted by door

Remember: Instruction must be student-centered, differentiated, and aligned with Next Generation Standards using Danielson's Framework.
